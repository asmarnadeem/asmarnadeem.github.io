About

Asmar Nadeem is a Research Fellow in Multimodal and Spatial AI at the Centre for Vision, Speech and Signal Processing (CVSSP), University of Surrey. His research builds machine learning systems that understand video the way people do, connecting what is seen and heard with why events happen. He works on causal and temporal reasoning for video, audio-visual question answering, video captioning, and multimodal large language models.

He is the first author of NarrativeBridge (ICLR 2025), CAD (WACV 2024), and SEM-POS (CVPR Workshops 2023), and has published across ICLR, WACV, CVPR Workshops, ECCV, ICME, and CVMP. His work introduces new benchmarks and architectures for causal video captioning and for aligning audio and video in question answering, several of which are released as open source.

Selected publications

NarrativeBridge: Enhancing Video Captioning with Causal-Temporal Narrative

Asmar Nadeem, Faegheh Sardari, Robert Dawes, Sameed Husain, Adrian Hilton, Armin Mustafa

International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2025 17 citations

Existing video captioning models describe what happens but not why. NarrativeBridge closes that gap with two contributions: the Causal-Temporal Narrative benchmark, containing the MSVD-CTN and MSR-VTT-CTN datasets whose captions encode cause and effect over time, and the Cause-Effect Network, a two stream architecture that encodes cause and effect dynamics separately before decoding a causally grounded caption.

Paper (ICLR 2025) Project page Code

CAD: Contextual Multi-Modal Alignment for Dynamic AVQA

Asmar Nadeem, Adrian Hilton, Robert Dawes, Graham Thomas, Armin Mustafa

IEEE/CVF Winter Conference on Applications of Computer Vision (WACV), 2024 20 citations

CAD is an end to end network for audio-visual question answering that aligns audio and visual signals at three levels: a parameter free stochastic contextual block for spatial alignment, a self supervised pre-training step for temporal alignment, and cross attention for semantic balancing. It improves over prior state of the art on the MUSIC-AVQA dataset by 9.4 percent on average.

Paper (WACV 2024) Code Video

SEM-POS: Grammatically and Semantically Correct Video Captioning

Asmar Nadeem, Adrian Hilton, Robert Dawes, Graham Thomas, Armin Mustafa

IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) Workshops, 2023 11 citations

SEM-POS introduces a global local fusion network that supervises captioning with distinct part of speech components, aligning visual features with language structure. It produces video captions that are both grammatically and semantically correct, reaching state of the art results on the MSVD and MSR-VTT benchmarks.

Frequently asked questions

Who is Asmar Nadeem?

Asmar Nadeem is a Research Fellow in Multimodal and Spatial AI at CVSSP, University of Surrey. He researches causal video understanding, audio-visual question answering, video captioning, and multimodal large language models, and is the first author of NarrativeBridge (ICLR 2025), CAD (WACV 2024), and SEM-POS (CVPR Workshops 2023).

What is NarrativeBridge?

NarrativeBridge (ICLR 2025), led by Asmar Nadeem, is a video captioning method that describes not only what happens in a video but why. It contributes the Causal-Temporal Narrative benchmark (MSVD-CTN and MSR-VTT-CTN) and the Cause-Effect Network, a two stream architecture that models cause and effect dynamics separately before decoding a causally grounded caption.

What is CAD and how does it improve audio-visual question answering?

CAD (WACV 2024) aligns audio and visual signals at the spatial, temporal, and semantic levels using a parameter free contextual block, a self supervised temporal alignment step, and cross attention. It improves over prior state of the art on the MUSIC-AVQA dataset by 9.4 percent on average.
